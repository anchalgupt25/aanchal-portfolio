The user problem

My role

NouriScan informs a parent's decision. It does not diagnose, prescribe, or pretend certainty when evidence is sparse.

Product pivot

Rebuilt NouriBot when fixed rules broke under real parent needs.

Static suggestion paths could not adapt to the different questions parents asked after the same scan. I moved to contextual LLM-generated guidance with a narrower role and stronger guardrails.

Trust design

Made “I don't know” a first-class product behavior.

Golden-dataset evaluations and confidence calibration exposed cases where a plausible response was more dangerous than no response. Sparse evidence now triggers an explicit limitation instead of false certainty.

Scope discipline

Held a retailer-link feature from MVP after inconsistent outputs.

The feature worked often—but not reliably across retailers. I chose a narrower trustworthy launch over a broader experience that could erode parent confidence.

Built with Claude Code, an agentic workflow, RAG, prompt engineering, evaluation datasets, confidence calibration, and explicit safe-failure behavior.

Product principles

Three beliefs that shape how I build AI products.

“

Design failure behavior before the happy path.

LLMs often fail plausibly. Uncertainty, refusal, and recovery need explicit product behavior, evaluation criteria, and ownership.

“

The right human intervention is a feature—not a fallback.

The goal is not maximum automation. It is placing judgment, review, or escalation exactly where it protects the user and the system.

“

Build to learn what should become product.

Hands-on prototypes expose model limits, UX friction, and hidden assumptions faster than polished strategy documents alone.
